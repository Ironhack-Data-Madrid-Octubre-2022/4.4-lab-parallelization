How to add to the community bulletin board
\n
\n
\n
The community bulletin board has 2 sections that can be used by Wikipedians for announcements: "Events and projects" and "WikiProject notices". In general, keep it concise (under 2 lines), refrain from fancy formatting, and new entries should be placed at the top of their section.\n
  • Before editing, make sure you are on Wikipedia:Community bulletin board, not Wikipedia:Community portal, where the board is transcluded.
    • \n
  • Events and projects: In this section, only organized events, projects, and/or competitions should be listed. These are organized by how often they occur:\n
    • The Yearly section is for uncommon events, like events that only occur every year, once, or irregularly. The Monthly section is for events that occur each month, or are always ongoing.
    • \n
  • WikiProject notices: In this section, any announcement, request for help or other notice from a WikiProject should be listed here.\n
    • Entries should be signed, and ordered from newest to oldest.
      • \n
    • Entries are to be removed after a period of 6 months.

Events and projects
\n

Yearly or infrequent events\n

\n
  • WikiProject Women in Green: GA Editathon. From October 1\xe2\x80\x9331, WikiProject Women in Green is hosting a virtual editathon event with the following goals: to improve, nominate, and review GA-class articles related to women and women\'s works (e.g., books, films), and to help editors build a greater capacity for bringing articles up to GA (Good Article) status. Both new and experienced participants are welcome! Instructions, resources, and one-on-one assistance will be available (plus a special barnstar). Sign up here.
    • \n
  • Wikipedia:WikiCup. The WikiCup is a championship that has taken place every year on Wikipedia since 2007. The Cup is played and won by skill of editing. The purpose of the Cup is to encourage content creation and improvement and make editing on Wikipedia more fun. There is a new round every month, until the current contest ends; below are the details.\n
    • May 1 to June 28 \xe2\x80\x93 32 contestants left.
      • \n
    • July 1 to August 29 \xe2\x80\x93 16 contestants left.
      • \n
    • September 1 to October 31 \xe2\x80\x93 8 finalists left, the contestant with the highest score will win!

Monthly or continuous events\n

\n
  • The Vital GA Drive is active from 1 September to 15 November 2022, with an aim to improve Land and Coffee articles to good article status. Join us now!
    • \n
  • Monthly contest, WikiProject Military history. The contest department of the Military history WikiProject aims to motivate increased quality in military history articles by offering a form of friendly competition for project members making improvements to them. The primary contest available is a simple rolling competition that awards points for improving articles. The contest runs from the first to last day of each month.
    • \n
  • Guild of Copy Editors\' editing drive . The November 2022 backlog drive is a one-month-long effort of the Guild of Copy Editors to reduce the backlog of articles that require copy editing; those carrying the {{copy edit}} tag (also {{awkward}}, {{copy edit section}}, {{inappropriate person}}, and {{copy edit inline}} and their redirects) or listed on the GOCE Requests page. It begins 1 November 2022, 00:00 (UTC), and ends 30 November 2022, 23:59 (UTC).

This is a loose challenge, a list will not be maintained here, but the components will function independently on each of the subpages. Will be updated every week or two.\n

Editing articles for web accessibility
\n

Web accessibility is the goal of making web pages easier to navigate and read. While this is primarily intended to assist those with disabilities, it can be helpful to all readers.\n

While editing articles, keep in mind the goal of making Wikipedia web accessible. All users, regardless of ability, should be able to read, navigate, and contribute to Wikipedia easily.\n

The Accessibility section of Wikipedia\'s Manual of Style (MOS) is a valuable resource that provides helpful guidelines. In addition, Accessibility dos and do nots is a quick summary of the most important guidelines for editors.\n

WikiProject Accessibility is a group of editors promoting better access for disabled and other users. For more information, such as what you can do to help, see the main project page.\n
