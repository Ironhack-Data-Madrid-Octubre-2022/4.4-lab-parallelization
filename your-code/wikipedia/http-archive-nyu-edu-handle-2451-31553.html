Title:\xc2\xa0

Data Science and Prediction

Authors:\xc2\xa0Dhar, Vasant
Issue Date:\xc2\xa029-May-2012
Series/Report no.:\xc2\xa0CeDER-12-01
Abstract:\xc2\xa0The world\'s data is growing more than 40% annually. Coupled with\nexponentially growing computing horsepower, this provides us with\nunprecedented basis for \'learning\' useful things from the data through\nstatistical induction without material human intervention and acting on\nthem. Philosophers have long debated the merits and demerits of\ninduction as a scientific method, the latter being that conclusions are\nnot guaranteed to be certain and that multiple and numerous models can\nbe conjured to explain the observed data. I propose that \'big data\'\nbrings a new and important perspective to these problems in that it\ngreatly ameliorates historical concerns about induction, especially if\nour primary objective is prediction as opposed to causal model\nidentification. Equally significantly, it propels us into an era of\nautomated decision making, where computers will make the bulk of\ndecisions because it is infeasible or more costly for humans to do so.\nIn this paper, I describe how scale, integration and most importantly,\nprediction will be distinguishing hallmarks in this coming era of Data\nScience.\' In this brief monograph, I define this newly emerging field\nfrom business and research perspectives.
